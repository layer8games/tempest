1 /*========================================================================
2 The Sprite, or rendering cell is the basic Geometric perimitive that any
3 game object or entity that is to be rendered will have. It will be the
4 OGL verticies that make up the position on the screen. While it will
5 never be called on its own, it will be part of the rendered game objects.
6 
7 Based on which RenderAs* function is call, the cell is be rendered as the
8 called primitive. The Sprite is not responsible for setting this up, the
9 Renderer is. The Sprite will just need to get its position to the Renderer
10 and the Renderer will set up all the Vec for OGL, and will then store
11 them in a container until the scene is drawn.
12 
13 The Sprite will also hold the texture information, once that is added to the
14 system. Basically it will hold all the data that needs to be given to the
15 Renderer in order for the Renderer to give that information to OGL.
16 
17 This is not free to use, and cannot be used without the express permission
18 of KillerWave.
19 
20 Written by Maxwell Miller
21 ========================================================================*/
